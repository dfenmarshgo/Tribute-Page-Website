Paul The Octopus

The predictor of Champions
Paul The Octopus was an octopus residing in Oberhausen, Germany who was used to predict the results of football matches. He came to prominence when he predicted the winners of each of the seven 2010 FIFA World Cup matches that the German team played. The opponents were Australia, Serbia, Ghana, England, Argentina, Spain and Uruguay.

    Some notable facts about Paul

  • Paul was hatched from an egg at the Sea Life Centre in Weymouth, England
  • Paul's name derived from the title of a poem by the German children's writer Boy Lornsen: Der Tintenfisch Paul Oktopus.
  • Paul's career began during the UEFA Euro 2008 tournament. Paul was presented with two clear plastic boxes, each containing food: a mussel or an oyster. Each container was marked with the flag of a team, Germany for one and ther opponent of Germany for the other. The box which Paul chose to eat from first was declared the prediction.
  • Paul's notoriety attracted criticism from the President of Iran, Mahmoud Ahmadinejad, who accused him of being a symbol of Western decadence and decay.
